Time Distribution

Recent years have been considerably safer for aviation, with fewer than 50 incidents every year between 2013 and 2016, compared to as many as 141 as recently as 2006. Over 10 incidents happened in eight of twelve months in 2006.
Annual fatalities have been less than 1000 since 1996. And we can see a trend of decreasing fatalities in recent years.

Incidents In relation to Flight Phase

Aircraft accident also have some relationship with the flight phase. Taxing and taking off contribute the most to the aircraft accident with a total of 187 count. Landing and cruising also have a large percentage. Accidents are distributed evenly among climbing, approaching, standing and descending, which is about 100 cases on average. The phase that is having the least cases is maneuvering and some unknown phases that is not recorded. The graph shows that the starting point and ending point are the most dangerous phases for aircraft.
